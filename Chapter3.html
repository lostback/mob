Real Flight Basics

Master these skills to ensure safe and successful flights.

+

Before each flight:

  • Check that the props are tight and undamaged.
  • Ensure the battery is securely connected and fully charged.
  • Inspect the frame for cracks or other damage.
  • Verify that the transmitter is bound to the drone and that goggles have a clear video feed.
+

Takeoff:

  • Arm the drone and slowly increase throttle until the drone lifts off.
  • Maintain a stable hover at about 2‚Äì3 feet before proceeding with any movements.

Landing:

  • Reduce throttle gradually as you bring the drone down.
  • Disarm the drone immediately after it touches the ground to prevent accidents.
+

Key tips for hovering:

  • Maintain a steady altitude by controlling throttle carefully.
  • Use small stick inputs to make adjustments to position.
  • Hover in place for 30 seconds as a practice drill.
+

Start practicing controlled movements:

  • Fly forward and backward while maintaining altitude.
  • Move side-to-side (left/right strafe) to build coordination.
  • Combine movements for smooth transitions, like forward and left at the same time.
+

Key tips for LOS flying:

  • Face the same direction as the drone to match its movements easily.
  • Practice turning the drone while maintaining awareness of its front/back orientation.
  • Switch to FPV goggles only after mastering LOS basics.
+

If you lose track of the drone's orientation:

  • Stop moving and hover to assess the situation.
  • Use small yaw adjustments to rotate the drone until it faces the correct direction.
  • If all else fails, disarm and retrieve the drone manually.

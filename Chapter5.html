Freestyle Basics

Take your flying to the next level with these freestyle techniques.

+

What is Acro Mode?

  • Unlike Angle or Horizon mode, Acro mode does not auto-level the drone.
  • This gives you full control over roll, pitch, and yaw, making freestyle tricks possible.
  • Start practicing Acro mode in the simulator before attempting it in real life.
+

How to Perform Rolls:

  • Increase throttle slightly to gain altitude.
  • Quickly move the right stick to the left or right to roll the drone.
  • Level out by centering the stick once the roll is complete.

How to Perform Flips:

  • Gain altitude before starting the flip.
  • Pull the right stick back (or push it forward) to flip the drone backward (or forward).
  • Center the stick to stabilize the drone after the flip.
+

The Split-S combines a roll and a dive:

  • Gain altitude and perform a half-roll to invert the drone.
  • Apply pitch (pull back on the stick) to dive down while completing the maneuver.
  • Level out before hitting the ground.

This is a useful trick for freestyle and changing directions quickly.

+

Combine tricks for smoother freestyle flying:

  • Link a roll into a flip for a more dynamic sequence.
  • Incorporate yaw to add spins during transitions.
  • Focus on timing and throttle control to keep movements fluid.
+

Tips for staying oriented:

  • Always be aware of the drone's orientation during tricks.
  • Practice recovering to a stable hover after each maneuver.
  • Use the environment (trees, landmarks) to maintain spatial awareness.
+

Freestyle can be risky; fly safely:

  • Perform tricks in open areas away from people and obstacles.
  • Start with small movements and build confidence before attempting complex tricks.
  • Always disarm the drone quickly after a crash to avoid motor damage.
